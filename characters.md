# Characters

Recurring cast used in the Session introduction notebooks. All fictional. Lindenfield Wealth Partners is a fictional mid-size Registered Investment Adviser (RIA) and serves as the setting across sessions.

## Maya Chen

Portfolio Manager at Lindenfield Wealth Partners. The protagonist across the course. In S1 she receives an AI-generated minimum-variance allocation for a Balanced-archetype client and has to decide whether to sign off. In S2 the same book has taken a twenty-one percent drawdown in August, tripped the firm's drawdown review threshold, and landed on the risk committee's agenda; Maya is the one answering for the process. She owns the client-facing sign-off decision.

## Lou

Maya's boss. Supervising principal at Lindenfield. Asks the gating question in S1 ("is this actually a good idea?") and tells Maya in S2 to walk into the risk committee with a number, not a derivation. He is in the committee room but Maya does the talking.

## Bud Fox

Builder of Lindenfield's AI intake tool (the agent that sorts clients into archetypes and produces the first-pass allocation). Answers for the design of the intake system when the S1 allocation is challenged in S2.

## Dr. Amara Okafor

Head of Quantitative Research at Lindenfield. Introduced in S2 as the source of the utility-based rebalancing framework that has been on the internal roadmap for two years. Her pitch reduces to three operational claims (signal, utility function, trigger rules) that become the three deliverables of the S2 lecture.
